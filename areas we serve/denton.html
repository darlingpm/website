Common Questions

Denton Property Management FAQs

How much does property management cost in Denton?
Our full management fee is 10% of monthly rent — right in line with the DFW average of 10–12%. Tenant placement is 75% of the first month's rent ($1,000 minimum). There are no fees while your property is vacant. For leasing-only services, the fee is 100% of the first month's rent.
What's the typical rent range for a 2-bed or 3-bed in Denton?
A 2-bed typically rents $1,200–$1,500 depending on location and proximity to UNT. A 3-bed ranges $1,550–$1,950. Properties near campus or in the Fry Street District command the higher end for student housing. We conduct a free rental analysis for every property to determine market-rate positioning.
How do you find tenants in Denton?
We syndicate every listing to Zillow, Apartments.com, Zumper, Realtor.com, and PadMapper in 24 hours. Our in-house photography and professional listing copy (written by our team) generate 3–4x more inquiries than average. For student housing, we also leverage university housing boards and student networks. Screening is rigorous and fair — we apply consistent criteria to every applicant, including student verification and guarantor requirements when appropriate.
How fast can you lease my vacant property in Denton?
Our average time to lease in Denton is 53 days. Our fastest placements happen in 21 days, especially for student housing near campus during recruitment season. Speed depends on pricing, condition, and tenant mix (student vs. professional). We'll give you an honest assessment during the onboarding consultation.
Do you handle emergency maintenance in Denton?
Yes. We have a vetted vendor network with 24/7 emergency response for water leaks, HVAC failures, electrical hazards, and security issues. Emergencies are dispatched within 1 hour. Routine repairs are scheduled within 3–7 business days and batched to control costs. Student properties require faster turnover — we prioritize quick resolution to minimize vacancy.
What if a tenant doesn't pay rent in Denton?
We send an automated reminder 3 days before the due date, then follow a structured delinquency protocol. For student tenants, we coordinate with guarantors (usually parents) early. We draft late-fee notices, formal demands, and — if necessary — begin eviction filings in Justice of the Peace court. We never shut off utilities or lock out tenants; we follow Texas Property Code §92.008 precisely. Legal consultation is recommended for contested evictions.
