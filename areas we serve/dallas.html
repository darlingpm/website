Why Invest Here

Why Dallas is a smart bet for rental investors

Dallas combines scale, diversity, and staying power that few markets can match. This is where institutional capital goes.

  • Largest renter pool in DFW (54% of households): More renters = less competition per property, better tenant quality, faster turnover, higher occupancy.
  • Fortune 500 headquarters + major employers: AT&T, Goldman Sachs, Parkland Health, UT Southwestern, Amazon logistics, JetBlue hub = recession-resistant tenant base. Job stability matters.
  • DART rail system: Transit-oriented properties near rail stations command rent premiums and attract commuters. White Rock, Uptown, Downtown, and emerging neighborhoods have rail access.
  • Diverse investment price points: Find cash-flowing $200K houses in emerging neighborhoods or $1M+ multifamily in prime locations. Scale across risk tiers simultaneously.
  • No Texas state income tax: Effective cap rates 0.5–1.0% higher than states with income tax. A $1M portfolio earns an extra $5K–$10K annually in pre-tax cash flow.
  • Year-round rental demand: Corporate relocations + DFW inflow + major universities (SMU, UTD) + military presence = constant pipeline of renters. No seasonal downturns.

Common Questions

Dallas property management FAQ

What's the typical rent for a 2-bed or 3-bed in Dallas?
Based on current market data (Mar 2026), rents vary significantly by neighborhood. A 2-bedroom typically runs $1,300–$1,800/month depending on area. A 3-bedroom ranges $1,800–$2,600/month. Lake Highlands and East Dallas offer affordable single-family homes; Uptown and M Streets command premiums. We'll provide a specific analysis for your property and neighborhood free of charge.
How long does it take to lease a vacant property?
Dallas's average market time is 62 days from listing to signed lease. With our professional photos, Innago syndication to 15+ major platforms, and targeted tenant screening, we typically fill properties in 45–65 days depending on neighborhood and price point. DART rail proximity and emerging neighborhoods lease faster. We keep you updated weekly with showing activity and interest.
What are your management fees?
Our full management fee is 10% of monthly rent — right in line with the DFW average of 10–12%. Tenant placement is 75% of the first month's rent ($1,000 minimum). There are no fees while your property is vacant. For leasing-only services, the fee is 100% of the first month's rent. No hidden fees, no surprises.
Do you handle emergency maintenance calls 24/7?
Yes. Any emergency (burst pipe, no heat, electrical hazard, break-in) is responded to within 1 hour by our preferred vendor network. Non-emergencies are scheduled within 24 hours. All work orders are logged in our system, and you get monthly reports. We prioritize cost control — vendors compete for our volume and understand our quality standards.
How do you screen tenants in Dallas?
We pull credit, criminal, and eviction reports through TransUnion/Equifax. Our screening criteria require income ≥3x monthly rent, a credit score of ≥650, zero evictions in the past 5 years, and positive rental references. We apply these standards identically to all applicants — Fair Housing compliant, documented, no exceptions. You'll know your tenant is vetted before lease signing.
When will I get paid, and what's included in the monthly report?
Rent is collected on the 1st of each month and disbursed by the 15th via Baselane. Your monthly report includes rent collected, itemized maintenance costs, net cash flow, tenant status, lease expiration dates, vacancy status, and year-over-year comparisons. Reports are delivered in writing and available in our online portal 24/7. We also conduct annual rent reviews to keep your property competitive.
