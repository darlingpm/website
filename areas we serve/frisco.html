Why Invest Here

Why Frisco is a smart bet for rental investors

Frisco isn't just growing — it's booming. What was a small town of 33,000 in 2000 is now a 230,000-person economic powerhouse with a national brand. For investors, that growth translates to sustained demand and premium rents.

  • Fastest-Growing Large City in America: Frisco's population grew 71% from 2010–2020 (U.S. Census), and growth continues with major master-planned communities like Phillips Creek Ranch and Fields still under development.
  • The Star District: The Dallas Cowboys World Headquarters and mixed-use entertainment complex is a national draw. Homes and rentals near The Star command premium prices and attract corporate tenants relocating for work.
  • PGA of America Headquarters: Relocated to Frisco in 2022 with a 600-acre campus, adding another anchor employer and raising the city's national profile — which in turn drives housing demand.
  • Top-Rated Frisco ISD: Multiple 10/10 GreatSchools-rated schools across the district draw families who are willing to pay premium rents for access — and tend to stay long-term.
  • Premium Rental Market: At $2,590 median rent, Frisco is 36% above the national average. Investors who acquire here benefit from some of the highest per-unit income in North Texas.
  • Corporate Relocations: Companies continue moving operations to Frisco's growing business corridors along the Dallas North Tollway, bringing high-income professional renters who need housing immediately upon arrival.
