Why Invest Here

Why Arlington is a smart bet for rental investors

Arlington combines entertainment-driven demand with affordable entry prices — a rare combination in North Texas.

  • 4th-Largest Rental Market in DFW: With 400,000+ residents and a 48% renter rate, Arlington offers one of the deepest tenant pools in North Texas. Lower vacancy risk and faster turnaround between tenants.
  • UT Arlington — 50,000+ Students: One of the largest universities in Texas creates permanent, year-round rental demand near campus. Student housing returns are among the most consistent in DFW.
  • AT&T Stadium & Globe Life Field: Sports tourism and entertainment employment create sustained economic activity that translates into stable rental demand across Arlington's submarkets.
  • Most Affordable Entry in Mid-Cities: At $300K median home price, Arlington offers the lowest entry point among major DFW employment hubs. Strong cash flow potential even at full financing.
  • GM Assembly Plant & Medical District: General Motors' 2.5M sq ft plant, Texas Health Resources, and Medical City Arlington Hospital provide blue-collar and healthcare employment driving stable rental demand.
  • Wide Price Range: From $180K entry-level homes to $600K+ lakefront estates in Viridian, Arlington offers investment opportunities across every price point and strategy.

Common Questions

Arlington property management FAQ

What are typical rent ranges in Arlington?
Arlington's 2-bedroom homes rent for $1,200–$1,550/month, and 3-bedrooms for $1,550–$2,100/month depending on location, age, and condition. Viridian waterfront properties and North Arlington homes command the higher end of the range.
How much does property management cost in Arlington?
Our full management fee is 10% of monthly rent — right in line with the DFW average of 10–12%. Tenant placement is 75% of the first month's rent ($1,000 minimum). There are no fees while your property is vacant. For leasing-only services, the fee is 100% of the first month's rent. See full pricing details →
Is UT Arlington a good driver of rental demand?
Yes. UT Arlington's 50,000+ students create year-round demand for rentals within 2–3 miles of campus. Proximity to UTA adds a reliable tenant pipeline that doesn't disappear when corporate employers downsize.
Does event traffic from AT&T Stadium help rental demand?
Indirectly, yes. The stadium, Globe Life Field, and Six Flags drive significant employment in hospitality, event services, and retail — providing a stable workforce renter base. The entertainment economy is Arlington's moat.
How does Arlington's entry price compare to other DFW markets?
Arlington's $300K median home price is significantly more affordable than Plano ($482K), Frisco ($620K), or McKinney ($520K). Investors get comparable tenant quality at a lower cost basis — meaning stronger cash-on-cash returns from day one.
