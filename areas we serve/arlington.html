Frequently Asked

What are typical rent ranges in Arlington?
Arlington's 2-bedroom homes rent for $1,200–$1,500/month, and 3-bedrooms for $1,600–$2,000/month depending on age, condition, and neighborhood. Student housing near UT rents on the lower end; professional enclaves command premium rates.
How much does property management cost?
Our full management fee is 10% of monthly rent — right in line with the DFW average of 10–12%. Tenant placement is 75% of the first month's rent ($1,000 minimum). There are no fees while your property is vacant. For leasing-only services, the fee is 100% of the first month's rent.
How does UT Arlington's student population benefit my rental?
42,000+ students create year-round demand for sub-$1,600 off-campus housing. Annual lease cycles mean predictable turnover and consistent tenant placement. Student renters typically stay 1-2 years (defined terms) and are renewal-friendly.
Can Arlington properties benefit from event-driven demand?
Yes. AT&T Stadium (Cowboys) and Globe Life Field (Rangers) events create hotel-alternative short-term rental demand. Event weeks can justify 30–50% premium rates on properly positioned properties near the stadiums.
How does Arlington's $300K median entry compare to other DFW markets?
Arlington's $300K median is 20–30% below Dallas ($420K), Plano ($520K), and Frisco ($580K). Lower entry means lower down payments and the ability to acquire more doors with the same capital. Ideal for volume investors.
